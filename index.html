EB Primer

APPLY

Hey UXer! This is UXSoc's short primer on our EB positions for next year. May these descriptions of each position serve you well in expressing interest and applying!

President

The Chief Evangelist

The President is in charge of establishing the vision for the organization and making sure it can be translated to a portfolio of projects and events during his/her term.

He/she is also in charge of monitoring or handling external affairs of the organization. He or she should be open to mentoring the next set of leaders for the organization.

Some responsibilities/tasks include coordinating with LIONS and other offices, partnering up with companies and organizations for design-led initiatives, constantly absorbing lessons, tools, and methods that can help the organization with its projects, and ensuring that the EB is happy and growing.

It’s recommended that the President has the skills of effective communication, persuasion, ability to handle conflict, good judgment and past experience on leadership and team management.

Executive Vice President

The Right Hand

The EVP is the right hand of the President.

He/she fills in the gaps the President cannot fill. The EVP understands that the President cannot do it alone, and neither can the organization.

You have more flexibility in making things happen. You pounce on opportunities the organization can undertake fearlessly — whether it be through multimedia, partnerships, branding, potential events, and more.

You are as concerned with how the organization performs and you double down on how we can become 10x. You are always thinking of the organization roadmap and execute what you think is best for the organization.

As the executive among the VPs, you are the manager. You are deeply concerned with each of their growth. You schedule individual consultations, hold honest conversations, and find the root of the matter whenever they do not perform well.

Your excellent work ethic, standards of quality, and can-do attitude takes the organization far more than it could be without you.

Secretary General

The Organizer

The SecGen is in charge of establishing organized file management and appropriate documentation.

Some responsibilities/tasks include setting up important internal meetings while following respective agendas, creating an organized Google Drive (or any other platform) available to the team, and upholding standards of quality for org documents.

It’s recommended that the SecGen is detail-oriented, a team player, a critical listener, and passionately champions quality standards. Past experiences on quick and effective note-taking is also recommended.

Vice President of User Experience Design

The Design Educator

The VP for UX Design is in charge of leading a team of designers in creating digital products for clients.

He or she also ensures that UX design is effectively taught throughout the organization.

It’s recommended that the VP for UX Design has past experience not only in product design but also profound knowledge in user research, visual design, and usability as well. The VP for UX Design works hand in hand with the VP for Consultancy in managing the UXDC team.

Vice President of Consultancy

The Client Manager

The VP for Consultancy is in charge of actively looking for clients who need design and development projects.

This is so that UXSoc officers can practice their UI/UX and dev’t skills. Together with the VP of UX Design, their department is the main source of income of UXSoc. Together they’re also in charge of providing resources to those who are just learning about UX.

The VP of Consultancy must be ready to pitch the org’s services, quote the right prices to clients, reasonably negotiate with them, and deliver top-notch output quickly.

Some responsibilities of the VP of Consultancy is to mentor officers and members on UX design and assigning projects to those who are fit for the role. He/she must be responsible in overseeing these projects to ensure happy officers and satisfied customers.

Vice President of Engineering

The Coding Sage

The VP for Engineering is in charge of transforming designs into digital masterpieces that are usable, useful, and delightful.

He is the chief technological officer that manages all the web development projects. He or she works hand in hand with the UXDC and ensures that the projects are moving forward as planned. This VP is also in charge of developing and honing the programming skills of his/her developers.

He or she should also have a solid understanding of how to incorporate business models into UX Design and development processes. He or she ensures that development fees are right and justifiable and that quality sites are produced.

It’s recommended that the VP for Engineering should have an in-depth knowledge and experience in using the git flow and programming front-end and back-end applications.

He or she should have a coding portfolio, meaning he or she has already worked on other projects before. Moreover, the VP for Engineering should also be meticulous in the code structure, style and infrastructure.

Vice President of Events

The Event Experience Designer

The VP of Events is in charge of making sure the programs and logistics of UXSoc's events are smooth and well-designed.

He/she is an efficient planner and organizer of events. He/she is familiar with Ateneo’s (rigorous) mechanisms with regards to venue reservations and project proposals. If not, he/she is ready to learn them, immerse in them, and give their soul to them.

He/she knows how to deploy project teams, manage expectations and motivations, and pick the right people for the right job.

Vice President of Human Resources

The Support Coach

The VP for HR is in charge of the recruitment, development, and happiness of all members of UXSoc.

Some responsibilities/tasks include ensuring a great and smooth RecWeek promotion and experience, coming up with initiatives and systems to track and increase member engagement, and managing initiatives to foster better member relations and better member development.

It’s recommended that the VP for HR have great writing skills and planning skills, and be an organized and systematic person. It’s also recommended that he/she have a passion for helping and supporting others, whether through sharing resources or for organizing fun activities.

Vice President of Branding

The Aesthetic Storyteller

The VP for Branding is responsible for maintaining the excellent quality of marketing collaterals and multimedia that UX Society produces.

These include illustration, graphic design, photography, and videography.

Some responsibilities include ensuring a successful RecWeek campaign, coming up with creative themes/concepts for various events and strategizing the execution through various social media channels to contribute to the organization’s existing brand identity.

It’s recommended that the VP for Branding has past experience with creating promotional materials and is a team player at all times. It is also important for the VP to be able to handle the unexpected and is reliable even at the last minute..

Vice President of Finance

The Money Lover

The VP of Finance keeps the organization running with enough cash.

He/she keeps the organization’s financials in check, and creates systems and processes to ensure money is properly managed going in and going out.

He/she sets systems to track where our finances go. This VP is obsessed with getting receipts, tallying expenses, and analyzing our financial health so that we know where we can better invest our money.

This VP is often collaborating with the UX Design department in order to secure payments from clients. It is not unnatural for him/her to be handling MoA’s and other similar documents. He/she is also able to keep track of expenses such as hosting and domain management fees, SSL certificates, and more.

Vice President of Marketing

The Strategic Campaigner

The VP of Marketing is in charge of ensuring UXSoc’s initiatives are properly marketed and reach a large, interested audience.

This includes handling social media blasts, helping coming up with campaigns for our social media accounts, and helping spread the word about UX and UXSoc.

He/she will help develop content and campaigns to make the organization more popular. If there are external partnerships as well, he/she will be in charge of liaising with the other organizations regarding these, with the help of the EVP and/or President.
